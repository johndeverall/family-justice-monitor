Media Curator Volunteer Role

Help us build the research foundations of Family Justice Monitor NZ

About Family Justice Monitor

Family Justice Monitor NZ is an initiative in formation to provide independent research and monitoring of New Zealand's family justice system. We are building an evidence-based organisation that combines rigorous research methodology with the lived experiences of affected families. Our work is guided by principles of independence, transparency, and credibility to inform policy discussions and improve outcomes for children and families.

The Role

We're seeking a volunteer Media Curator to help us develop and build a comprehensive archive of media coverage and public discourse about New Zealand's family justice system. This role is foundational to our research mission—working together to create a systematic record of how the family justice system is discussed, understood, and experienced in the public sphere.

You'll help shape how we approach media documentation and analysis.

What You'll Do

  • Systematically collect media articles, news reports, opinion pieces, and commentary about the family court system in New Zealand from diverse sources and perspectives
  • Catalogue and categorise articles objectively by relevant themes (e.g., custody outcomes, court processes, systemic issues, policy changes, lived experiences)
  • Create standardised records including source, date, key themes, and brief factual summaries
  • Maintain an organised database that supports future research and analysis
  • Ensure comprehensive coverage across different media outlets, perspectives, and viewpoints
  • Document media trends and patterns in coverage over time
  • Flag significant developments in family justice policy, practice, or public discussion

Time Commitment

  • Approximately 3-5 hours per week
  • Flexible schedule - work at times that suit you
  • Initial commitment of 3 months, with option to continue

Skills & Attributes We're Looking For

  • Strong research and information literacy skills, or eagerness to develop them
  • Attention to detail and systematic approach to documentation
  • Commitment to objectivity—ability to catalogue content across all perspectives without bias
  • Familiarity with New Zealand media landscape (helpful but not essential)
  • Interest in family law, social research, or evidence-based policy
  • Ability to work independently while collaborating on developing best practices
  • Basic computer skills (comfortable using spreadsheets or databases)
  • Understanding that research requires documenting diverse viewpoints, not selecting evidence to support predetermined conclusions
  • Willingness to help develop and refine our research processes as we grow

What You'll Gain

  • Meaningful contribution to building independent research capacity on family justice in New Zealand
  • Opportunity to help develop systematic approaches to media analysis and documentation
  • Understanding of how the family court system operates and is discussed in public discourse
  • Chance to help build the research foundations of a new independent organisation from the ground up
  • Portfolio/CV enhancement for those interested in law, research, social policy, or journalism
  • Collaborative environment where your ideas about research methods and approaches will be valued

About Us

Family Justice Monitor NZ is currently in formation as an independent research and monitoring initiative. We're building our founding membership and establishing governance structures for evidence-based research. This is an opportunity to help establish rigorous research practices from the ground up, working collaboratively to develop effective methods and approaches.

How to Apply

Please send a brief expression of interest (no formal cover letter needed) to contact@familycourtmonitor.org.nz including:

  • Why you're interested in this role
  • Relevant experience or skills
  • Your availability

We welcome applications from people with lived experience of the family justice system, as well as those with backgrounds in research, law, journalism, social sciences, or related fields. A commitment to objective, systematic research practice is essential.
