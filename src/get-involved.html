Get Involved

Your voice and support can help create a family justice system that truly serves families and protects children. As we establish this initiative, there are several ways to contribute.

Become a Founding Member

We are recruiting founding members to establish Family Justice Monitor NZ as an Incorporated Society. A minimum of 10 founding members is required to incorporate under the Incorporated Societies Act 2022.

As a Founding Member, you will:

  • Help adopt the Society's constitution and rules
  • Elect the inaugural Committee
  • Shape the organisation's strategic direction and operational framework
  • Be part of establishing independent oversight of NZ's family justice system

We are seeking individuals with diverse backgrounds, particularly those with expertise in:

  • Legal / policy expertise (former judges, family lawyers, or academics)
  • Social work / psychology
  • Māori / Pacific representation and cultural knowledge
  • Data / analytics and research methodology
  • Public interest voice (journalism, NGO leadership)
  • Governance or not-for-profit experience
  • Lived experience of the family justice system

Share Your Experience

Have you or your whānau been affected by the family justice system? Your story matters. We provide safe, confidential ways for families to share their experiences and contribute to our research.

What We're Looking For:

  • Direct experiences with family justice proceedings
  • Perspectives from parents, caregivers, and (where appropriate) young people
  • Insights from professionals working with affected families
  • Information about both positive experiences and areas needing improvement

How We Protect Privacy & Comply with Legal Requirements:

  • All participation is voluntary and can be anonymous
  • We comply with Section 11B of the Family Courts Act 1980, which prohibits publication of identifying information about children and vulnerable persons involved in Family Court proceedings
  • All case studies and stories will be fully anonymised, with identifying details removed or replaced
  • We will not publish any information that could identify parties to Family Court proceedings without court permission
  • Sensitive information is stored securely
  • You can withdraw your participation at any time
