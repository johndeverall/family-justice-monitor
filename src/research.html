Our Research

We conduct rigorous, independent research to understand how New Zealand's family justice system impacts families, children, and society. Our evidence-based approach combines quantitative analysis with qualitative case studies to provide comprehensive insights.

Research Focus Areas

Court Processes & Outcomes

How does the family justice system function in practice? We examine waiting times, consistency of decision-making, access to justice, and whether outcomes align with stated goals.

Impact on Children

What are the short and long-term outcomes for children affected by family justice decisions? We look at wellbeing indicators, educational outcomes, mental health, and stability.

Parental Experiences

How do parents experience the family justice process? We examine accessibility, fairness perceptions, legal representation issues, and the impact on parent-child relationships.

Cultural & Demographic Patterns

Are there disparities in how different communities experience the family justice system? We analyze patterns across ethnicity, socioeconomic status, geography, and other factors.

System Costs & Efficiency

What are the broader social and economic costs associated with family justice proceedings? We examine both direct costs and downstream impacts on mental health services, social support systems, and productivity.

Legal Industry Practices

How do lawyers, psychologists, and other professionals interact with the system? We examine professional practices, financial incentives, and their impact on family outcomes.

Our Methodology

Mixed Methods Approach

We combine quantitative analysis of court data and social outcomes with qualitative research including interviews, case studies, and surveys of affected families.

Rigorous Standards

All research undergoes ethical review and follows established social science research protocols. Where possible, we seek peer review of our findings.

Lived Experience Integration

We center the voices of those affected by the family justice system, ensuring research questions and interpretations reflect real-world experiences.

Independent Verification

We seek to verify findings through multiple data sources and welcome academic collaboration and review.

Current Research Projects

We are currently establishing our research program and building partnerships with academic institutions and community organizations. Initial research projects will focus on:

  • Mapping the landscape of family justice outcomes in New Zealand
  • Gathering lived experience narratives from affected families
  • Analyzing demographic patterns in family justice proceedings
  • Reviewing international best practices and comparative systems
