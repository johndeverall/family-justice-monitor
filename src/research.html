Research Vision

We intend to conduct rigorous, independent research to understand how New Zealand's family justice system impacts families, children, and society. Our evidence-based approach will combine quantitative analysis with qualitative case studies to provide comprehensive insights.

Intended Research Focus Areas

Court Processes & Outcomes

How does the family justice system function in practice? We will examine waiting times, consistency of decision-making, access to justice, and whether outcomes align with stated goals.

Impact on Children

What are the short and long-term outcomes for children affected by family justice decisions? We will look at wellbeing indicators, educational outcomes, mental health, and stability.

Parental Experiences

How do parents experience the family justice process? We will examine accessibility, fairness perceptions, legal representation issues, and the impact on parent-child relationships.

Cultural & Demographic Patterns

Are there disparities in how different communities experience the family justice system? We will analyse patterns across ethnicity, socioeconomic status, geography, and other factors.

System Costs & Efficiency

What are the broader social and economic costs associated with family justice proceedings? We will examine both direct costs and downstream impacts on mental health services, social support systems, and productivity.

Legal Industry Practices

How do lawyers, psychologists, and other professionals interact with the system? We will examine professional practices, financial incentives, and their impact on family outcomes.

Intended Methodology

Mixed Methods Approach

We will combine quantitative analysis of court data and social outcomes with qualitative research including interviews, case studies, and surveys of affected families.

Rigorous Standards

All research will undergo ethical review and follow established social science research protocols. Where possible, we will seek peer review of our findings.

Lived Experience Integration

We will centre the voices of those affected by the family justice system, ensuring research questions and interpretations reflect real-world experiences.

Independent Verification

We will seek to verify findings through multiple data sources and welcome academic collaboration and review.

Current Research Projects

We are currently establishing our research programme and building partnerships with academic institutions and community organisations. Initial research projects will focus on:

  • Mapping the landscape of family justice outcomes in New Zealand
  • Gathering lived experience narratives from affected families
  • Analysing demographic patterns in family justice proceedings
  • Reviewing international best practices and comparative systems
