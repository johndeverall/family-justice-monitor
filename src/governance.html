Governance Structure

Family Justice Monitor NZ will be established as an Incorporated Society under the Incorporated Societies Act 2022, with plans to subsequently apply for charitable status with Charities Services.

This structure ensures democratic governance, accountability to members, and alignment with New Zealand's legal framework for not-for-profit organisations.

Incorporated Society Structure

Founding Members

We are currently recruiting founding members who will form the initial Society. A minimum of 10 members is required to incorporate. Founding members will:

  • Adopt the Society's constitution and rules
  • Elect the inaugural Committee
  • Establish the organisation's operational framework
  • Guide the initial strategic direction

The Committee (Governance Body)

Once the Society is incorporated, members will elect a Committee to provide strategic oversight and ensure we remain true to our mission while maintaining the highest standards of independence and integrity.

Intended Committee Composition (7-9 officers)

We are seeking to bring together diverse expertise and perspectives through our founding membership:

Independent Chair

Governance experience with legal or social policy background

Lived Experience Representatives

Two individuals with direct experience of the family justice system, representing different perspectives

Legal Expert

Family law practitioner or academic

Child Welfare Specialist

Expert in child development and protection

Research Expert

Social science researcher or methodologist

Māori Representative

Ensuring Te Tiriti obligations and Māori perspectives are centred

Pacific Peoples Representative

Representing Pacific communities' experiences and needs

Financial/Operational Expert

Ensuring sound organisational management

Why This Structure Matters

Democratic Governance

The Incorporated Society structure ensures democratic accountability to members and transparent decision-making processes.

Balance & Independence

Our intended Committee structure deliberately balances multiple perspectives—legal expertise, lived experience, cultural knowledge, and research rigour—ensuring no single viewpoint dominates our work.

Lived Experience at the Centre

We believe those who have navigated the family justice system must have a voice in governance, not just in consultations. Their insights are invaluable to understanding real-world impacts.

Cultural Responsiveness

Māori and Pacific families are disproportionately represented in family justice proceedings. Our governance structure will ensure their perspectives inform our work from the ground up.

Expertise & Credibility

Our Committee's legal, research, and child welfare expertise will ensure our findings are rigorous, credible, and useful to policymakers and practitioners.

Pathway to Charitable Status

Once incorporated, we will apply for charitable status with Charities Services, enabling tax-deductible donations and demonstrating our commitment to public benefit.

Future Advisory Council

We plan to establish an Advisory Council that will provide specialist expertise without governance responsibility. We will seek members including:

  • Former family court judges and lawyers
  • Social workers and family therapists
  • Domestic violence specialists
  • Children's advocates
  • Mental health professionals
  • Academic researchers in family law and child outcomes
  • Representatives from related NGOs and community organisations

Once established, the Advisory Council will meet quarterly to provide input on research priorities, methodology, and policy positions.

Ethical Framework & Legal Compliance

We are committed to operating under strict ethical guidelines and legal requirements:

  • Legal Compliance - Full compliance with Section 11B of the Family Courts Act 1980, which prohibits publication of identifying information about children and vulnerable persons in Family Court proceedings
  • Confidentiality - Protecting the privacy and safety of families who share their experiences through complete anonymisation of all case studies and stories
  • Do No Harm - Ensuring our work doesn't re-traumatise vulnerable people or breach legal protections
  • Informed Consent - Clear communication about how information will be used and our legal obligations regarding anonymisation
  • Independence - Transparent about funding and potential conflicts of interest
  • Rigorous Methodology - Committed to research best practices and peer review

Transparency & Accountability

Once operational, we will publish:

  • Annual reports detailing our activities and findings
  • Committee meeting summaries (respecting confidentiality where needed)
  • Financial statements and funding sources
  • Society constitution and rules
  • Research methodology and data sources
  • Our governance policies and ethical guidelines
