Our Governance Structure

Family Justice Monitor NZ operates under a robust governance structure designed to ensure independence, credibility, and effectiveness in our monitoring and advocacy work.

Board of Directors

Our Board provides strategic oversight and ensures we remain true to our mission while maintaining the highest standards of independence and integrity.

Board Composition (7-9 members)

Our Board brings together diverse expertise and perspectives:

Independent Chair

Governance experience with legal or social policy background

Lived Experience Representatives

Two individuals with direct experience of the family justice system, representing different perspectives

Legal Expert

Family law practitioner or academic

Child Welfare Specialist

Expert in child development and protection

Research Expert

Social science researcher or methodologist

Māori Representative

Ensuring Te Tiriti obligations and Māori perspectives are centred

Pacific Peoples Representative

Representing Pacific communities' experiences and needs

Financial/Operational Expert

Ensuring sound organisational management

Why This Structure Matters

Balance & Independence

Our Board deliberately balances multiple perspectives—legal expertise, lived experience, cultural knowledge, and research rigor—ensuring no single viewpoint dominates our work.

Lived Experience at the Centre

We believe those who have navigated the family justice system must have a voice in governance, not just in consultations. Their insights are invaluable to understanding real-world impacts.

Cultural Responsiveness

Māori and Pacific families are disproportionately represented in family justice proceedings. Our governance structure ensures their perspectives inform our work from the ground up.

Expertise & Credibility

Our Board's legal, research, and child welfare expertise ensures our findings are rigorous, credible, and useful to policymakers and practitioners.

Advisory Council

Our Advisory Council provides specialist expertise without governance responsibility. Members include:

  • Former family court judges and lawyers
  • Social workers and family therapists
  • Domestic violence specialists
  • Children's advocates
  • Mental health professionals
  • Academic researchers in family law and child outcomes
  • Representatives from related NGOs and community organisations

The Advisory Council meets quarterly to provide input on research priorities, methodology, and policy positions.

Ethical Framework

We operate under strict ethical guidelines:

  • Confidentiality - Protecting the privacy and safety of families who share their experiences
  • Do No Harm - Ensuring our work doesn't re-traumatize vulnerable people
  • Informed Consent - Clear communication about how information will be used
  • Independence - Transparent about funding and potential conflicts of interest
  • Rigorous Methodology - Committed to research best practices and peer review

Transparency & Accountability

We publish:

  • Annual reports detailing our activities and findings
  • Board meeting summaries (respecting confidentiality where needed)
  • Financial statements and funding sources
  • Research methodology and data sources
  • Our governance policies and ethical guidelines
